What cookies and similar technologies are

Cookies are small text files that a website can place on your device when you visit it; they are widely used to make websites work, to remember choices, or to collect information about how a site is used. Similar technologies include browser storage mechanisms such as localStorage and sessionStorage, which let a website save small pieces of information directly in your browser without using a traditional cookie. Cookies and storage can be set by the website you are visiting (called "first-party") or by a different organisation whose content or service is embedded in the page (called "third-party"). They can also be "session" (deleted when you close the browser) or "persistent" (kept until they expire or you remove them).

Technical preference stored in your browser (localStorage)

When you make a choice on the cookie banner (for example, acknowledging or accepting the notice), the Site may save that choice locally in your browser using localStorage so that the banner does not keep reappearing on every page or visit. This is a small technical preference: it stays on your own device, it is not a tracking cookie, and it is not transmitted to AwakerHz or used to identify you. You can clear this stored preference at any time by clearing your browser's site data for awakerhz.com, after which the banner may appear again on your next visit.

How to manage or disable cookies

You are in control of cookies and similar technologies through your own browser. Most browsers let you view the cookies and site data stored, delete them, and block or limit cookies (including third-party cookies) either generally or for specific sites. You can usually find these controls in your browser's settings under a section named "Privacy", "Cookies", or "Site data". You can also use private or incognito browsing, or browser extensions that block third-party content, to further limit what is set. Please note that if you block all cookies or third-party content, some features may not work as intended; for example, embedded YouTube videos may not play correctly. For step-by-step instructions, refer to the help pages of your specific browser (for example Chrome, Firefox, Safari or Edge).
